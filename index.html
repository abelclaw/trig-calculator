1 What is a Function?

Imagine a machine. You put a number in one side, something happens inside, and a number comes out the other side. That's a function.

A function is a rule that takes each input and gives back exactly one output. No surprises, no randomness — the same input always gives the same output.

We write functions like this: f(x) = 2x. This means "the function f takes a number x and gives back 2 times x." The x is the input, and f(x) is the output.

Seeing a function as a graph

Every function can be drawn as a picture. The input (x) goes along the bottom, and the output goes up the side. Each point on the curve says "when the input is here, the output is there."

Move your mouse across the graph
On a graph, a function means: every vertical line crosses the curve at most once. If a vertical line hits the curve twice, the graph is not a function (because one input would give two outputs).

3 Inverse Functions: Undoing a Function

An inverse function undoes what the original function did. It's like going backwards through the machine to figure out what the input was.

Think of it like this:

  • Multiply by 2 is undone by divide by 2
  • Add 5 is undone by subtract 5
  • Square is undone by square root

An inverse function undoes what the original function did — like how division undoes multiplication, or how subtraction undoes addition.

5 Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean Theorem is one of the most famous formulas in all of math. It tells us how the three sides of a right triangle are related:

a² + b² = c²

Here, c is the hypotenuse (the longest side, opposite the right angle), and a and b are the two legs.

Try it: Adjust the legs and see the squares
3
4

3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5²

The classic example is the 3-4-5 triangle: 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5². Other common Pythagorean triples include 5-12-13 and 8-15-17.

In any right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides: a² + b² = c².

6 Similar Triangles

Two triangles are similar if they have the same angles. This is called AA similarity (Angle-Angle) — if two angles match, the third must also match (since angles in a triangle always add to 180°).

Similar triangles have the same shape but can be different sizes. Their corresponding sides are always in the same ratio:

7 Area & Perimeter

Area measures the space inside a shape. Perimeter measures the distance around the outside. Each shape has its own formula:

  • Triangle: A = ½bh
  • Rectangle: A = lw
  • Parallelogram: A = bh
  • Trapezoid: A = ½(a+b)h
  • Circle: A = πr²

8 Circles

A circle is defined by its center and radius (r). Every point on the circle is exactly r units from the center.

  • Radius (r): distance from center to edge
  • Diameter (d): 2r — distance across the circle through center
  • Circumference (C): 2πr — distance around the circle
  • Area (A): πr²
  • Arc length: (angle/360) × 2πr

A circle's circumference and area both depend on the radius. Pi (π ≈ 3.14159) is the ratio of circumference to diameter.

9 Transformations

Transformations change the position or orientation of a shape on the coordinate plane. The main rigid transformations are:

  • Translation: slide the shape without rotating
  • Reflection: flip the shape over a line (mirror image)
  • Rotation: turn the shape around a point

10 Coordinate Geometry

The coordinate plane lets us use algebra to solve geometry problems. Three key formulas connect coordinates to distances and directions:

  • Distance: d = √((x&sub2;−x&sub1;)² + (y&sub2;−y&sub1;)²)
  • Midpoint: ((x&sub1;+x&sub2;)/2, (y&sub1;+y&sub2;)/2)
  • Slope: m = (y&sub2;−y&sub1;)/(x&sub2;−x&sub1;)

Notice how the distance formula is really just the Pythagorean theorem! The horizontal difference is one leg, the vertical difference is the other, and the distance is the hypotenuse.

11 Quadrilaterals & Polygon Angles

Quadrilaterals are four-sided polygons. Each type has special properties:

  • Rectangle: 4 right angles, opposite sides equal & parallel
  • Rhombus: 4 equal sides, opposite angles equal
  • Square: 4 right angles AND 4 equal sides
  • Trapezoid: exactly one pair of parallel sides

For any polygon with n sides, the interior angles always add up to (n−2) × 180°.

Parallelogram Properties

A parallelogram has both pairs of opposite sides parallel. This gives it several guaranteed properties:

  • Opposite sides are equal in length
  • Opposite angles are equal
  • Consecutive angles are supplementary (add to 180°)
  • Diagonals bisect each other (cut each other in half)
Every rectangle is a parallelogram. Every rhombus is a parallelogram. Every square is both. This means squares and rectangles inherit ALL parallelogram properties.

13 Quadrilateral Areas

Each type of quadrilateral has its own area formula. The key is knowing which measurements you need.

Area Formulas

  • Rectangle: A = length × width
  • Parallelogram: A = base × height (height is perpendicular to base, not the slant side!)
  • Trapezoid: A = ½(b&sub1; + b&sub2;) × h (average the two parallel sides, then multiply by height)
  • Rhombus: A = ½ × d&sub1; × d&sub2; (half the product of the diagonals)
  • Kite: A = ½ × d&sub1; × d&sub2; (same formula as rhombus!)
The parallelogram area uses the perpendicular height, not the slant side. Imagine dropping a vertical line from the top to the base — that's the height.

Trapezoid Area: Why Average the Bases?

Think of it this way: a trapezoid is like a rectangle whose top was pushed to a different width. The "average" of the two bases gives you the width of an equivalent rectangle with the same area.

For rhombus and kite, the area formula A = ½d&sub1;d&sub2; works because the perpendicular diagonals divide the shape into four right triangles that together fill exactly half the bounding rectangle.

14 Volume & Surface Area

Volume measures the amount of 3D space inside a solid shape. Surface area measures the total area of all its outer faces.

  • Rectangular Prism: V = lwh, SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)
  • Cylinder: V = πr²h, SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
  • Cone: V = ⅓πr²h, SA = πr² + πr√(r²+h²)
  • Sphere: V = &frac43;πr³, SA = 4πr²
